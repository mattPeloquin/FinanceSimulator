Explore specific paths

A 3D plot of representative paths from the 5th to 65th percentiles. Column height represents portfolio balance, color reflects the annual real market return for that year (Red = crash, Green = boom). Drag to rotate, scroll to zoom, right-drag to pan. Single-click a column to pin that simulation and mouse over each year for values; click the column again or empty space to release. Double-click a column to explore ~200 nearby simulations; double-click again to return.

Sequence Risk: IRR vs Average Return

Each dot is one simulation. Dots below the dashed diagonal were hurt by early bad years — their money-weighted IRR trails their time-weighted average return; dots above it got lucky early. Paths in the lower-right had good average returns but still fell short of the plan: sequence risk in its purest form. Click a dot to see that path's withdrawals against its plan.

Withdrawal Heatmap

Built from every simulation run, not a sample. Each column is one simulation (or a narrow band of adjacent ones), ranked left-to-right by lifetime withdrawal across the Show from/to percentile window and rebinned to fill the chart width; rows are years, starting at the bottom — the Early years slider stretches the first rows taller. By default, color compares each year's withdrawal to your plan (orange = cut, teal = boost). Switch to vs Median to compare against that year's median across the runs shown (orange = below, teal = above), or Amount to show absolute withdrawal dollars over the from/to window (orange = low, teal = high). When columns are averaged bands, Speed replays random runs continuously and the Run slider scrubs individual-run snapshots, so you see the real spread rather than a smoothed mean. Runs outside the shown percentile window are not drawn. Click a column to see that path's withdrawals against its plan.
