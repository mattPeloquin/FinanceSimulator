Spending Over Time

Stage how your base withdrawal changes over retirement. Each tier's annual real change compounds continuously (negative = spend more now, less later). When a tier has an extra withdrawal, that % applies only to the extra (the core base stays flat for those years); when extra is 0, the % compounds the base instead. Add tiers to stage different patterns — the last tier applies to all remaining years. Remove all but one tier for a flat schedule. When Find Best Plan includes this section, it tunes only the first tier's extra withdrawal.

Major Events

Model expected cash events outside your spending schedule — selling a house or business, inheritance, reverse-mortgage draws, or known large payments. Positive amounts add to the portfolio; negative amounts are extra payments on top of that year's plan. Start year is year 1 of retirement. Leave Years blank for a one-time event, or enter how many consecutive years it repeats. Not used with a Specific List — bake those into your list instead. Events appear as dots on the schedule chart below.

Gifting

Add an extra gift withdrawal in years when your portfolio balance (after growth and your regular withdrawal or deposit) exceeds the tier's balance threshold and that year's spending fully met your plan — including any glide-path surplus, so a temporary market cut that glide replaces does not block the gift. Add tiers to stage different gifts over time — the last tier applies to all remaining years. Set Gift to 0 (or leave Balance blank) on early tiers to defer gifting to later years. Remove all tiers for no gifting. The chart below shows a dotted gift ceiling above your planned withdrawal.

Dynamic Adjustments & Guardrails

Market adjustment

Adjust withdrawal based on real market return (see chart). At the Expected return the plan is on track, so its adjustment is normally 0. When the balance is above the No cut threshold, downward adjustments are skipped — a bad market year never cuts spending while the portfolio is still ahead. Max boost drawdown limits how large a high-market boost can be so the balance after that year’s spending (except glide) does not fall more than this percent below the year’s starting balance — negative values require growth instead (e.g. −1% = finish at least 1% above start). Blank = no limit; only the market boost is reduced, never your base plan.

Balance adjustment

Smoothly scale your total withdrawal based on portfolio balance: below the floor, spending ramps down toward the max cut (reached at $0); above the ceiling, it ramps up by the boost rate for every extra multiple of the ceiling — with no upper cap. Between the two, spending is unchanged. Floor and Ceiling stay as Easy Mode (or you) set them. When Find Best Plan includes this section, it tunes max cut and boost rate only (never 0%) against those fixed thresholds — depth scales with Risk Tolerance over the first 0–20%.

Glide-path spend-down

Actively spend surplus down toward a target ending balance. Each year the simulator computes the balance still needed to fund your remaining plan and land on the target at the horizon; after any eligible gift is paid, when the portfolio still sits above that glide path, the spend rate share of the remaining surplus is withdrawn on top of the plan. The glide path declines toward the target as the horizon shrinks, so extra spending concentrates in the later years of comfortable paths and never cuts below your plan. Slide spend timing toward later to hold surplus back for late retirement — it raises the glide path so the early years stay invested, which helps plans that would otherwise dip to their minimum withdrawals mid-retirement. When Find Best Plan includes this section, it sets the target from Find Best Plan's Target Ending Balance and tunes the spend rate.
