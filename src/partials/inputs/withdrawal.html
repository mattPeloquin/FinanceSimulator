Major Events

Model expected cash events outside your spending schedule — selling a house or business, inheritance, reverse-mortgage draws, or known large payments. Positive amounts add to the portfolio; negative amounts are extra payments on top of that year's plan. Start year is year 1 of retirement. Leave Years blank for a one-time event, or enter how many consecutive years it repeats. Not used with a Specific List — bake those into your list instead. Gifting

Add extra gift withdrawals in years when spending is above the trigger adjustment from plan. Gifts pay only when the portfolio (after growth and your regular withdrawal or deposit) exceeds Balance >. The gift amount scales from 0% at the trigger to 100% at the target. Leave both blank to have the gift pay 100% if plan and balance are exceeded. Add tiers to shape giving over time, e.g., use a -100% target and trigger to ensure gifts for a period of time.

Balance adjustment

Scale total withdrawal based on portfolio balance: below the floor, spending ramps down toward the max cut; above the ceiling, it ramps up by the boost rate for every extra multiple of the ceiling — with no upper cap. Between the two, spending is unchanged.

Glide-path spend-down

Actively spend surplus down toward a target ending balance. Each year the simulator computes the balance still needed to fund your remaining plan and land on the end target. When the portfolio still sits above that glide path, the spend rate share of the remaining surplus is withdrawn. Slide spend timing toward later to hold surplus back for late retirement.
